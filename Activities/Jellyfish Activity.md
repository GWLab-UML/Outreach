# Jellyfish Experiment 

## Materials:
- Notebooks
- Magnifying glass
- Food dye
- Plastic bags 
- Mason Jars 
- Flask to observe jellyfish
- Beaker to transport jellyfish
- Mini Microscopes
- Petri dishes
- Colored paper
- Brine shrimp
- Jellyfish
- Scissors
- Thread/string
- Glitter
- Salt water to hold/transport jellyfish
- Pens/pencils

## Activity:
3 Stations (~15 minutes at each station)
### Craft Station
1. [Make jellyfish](https://www.pbs.org/parents/crafts-and-experiments/make-an-upcycled-jellyfish) out of plastic bags and mason jars
2. Talk about animals that might eat jellyfish (sea turtles) and how they mistake plastic bags for jellyfish
3. Encourage recycling plastic bags at grocery store

### Experiment Station
1. What is an experiment? A hypothesis?
2. Kids describe how the jellyfish move on their own with no changes
	- Side to side?
	- Up and down?
3. Record observations under different conditions
	- Changes to light (dark vs bright)
	- Changes to color paper under jellyfish
	- Changes to sound
	- Changes to movement (kids tap once or twice on side of petri dish)
4. Feed jellyfish (brine shrimp)

### Microscope Station
Kids take turns using microscope and drawing jellyfish
